Introducing Tendermint

The completely decentralized consensus engine.

Satoshi created a spark with Bitcoin, but the future is in Tendermint.

Research in decentralized consensus algorithms has been in progress since the early '80s. Today we have the ubiquitous and powerful computing network to run a decentralized blockchain ledger, and thanks to Bitcoin and the cryptocurrency community all the pieces are in place to redefine the nature of finance.

What makes Tendermint special?

At today’s Bitcoin prices and reward schedule, miners are rewarded on the order of $1,000,000 a day to secure the blockchain ledger — and a significant portion of that money is wasted on electricity.

Proof-of-work based consensus algorithms are slow, requiring up to an hour (or more for large transactions) to fully confirm a payment to prevent double-spending, and the forking nature of the blockchain makes application difficult to implement.

Tendermint solves these problems because it solves the long-standing “nothing at stake” problem. The solution is comprised of two parts.

First, we have a byzantine fault tolerant consensus algorithm.

In particular we choose as the algorithm framework, the DLS algorithm for its simplicity and ability to tolerate arbitrary disruptions in the network. This algorithm doesn’t incentivize nodes to waste electricity. Instead, it requires nodes to securely sign messages while participating in a round-based protocol.

Tendermint introduces a few innovations on top of the DLS algorithm to make it suitable for completely decentralized P2P gossip networks, just like Bitcoin.

Second, we create an incentive for nodes to behave in a way that makes blockchain forks extremely unlikely.

To do this, participants post a surety bond before becoming a validator that participates in the consensus process. To get the surety bond back validators must wait a relatively long duration of time.

If the validator causes a blockchain fork within this time bubble, he/she loses all of his/her surety bond. This creates a rolling checkpointing system that is updated in a completely decentralized fashion with no central authority.

Tendermint vs Bitcoin

Tendermint Bitcoin
Maximum security — backed by underlying coins Difficult to quantify — depends on extrinsic factors
Incentivizes privacy — powered by cryptography Incentivizes hashing — powered by wasteful energy expenditure
Immediate confirmations — ideal for application development Blockchain forks often — difficult to build upon
Compatible with an ecosystem of secure blockchains — ideal for sidechain innovation Only supports one secure blockchain globally — requires brittle merged mining

A technology & a community

Tendermint is both a decentralized organization as well as a foundational technology.

We believe that the future is comprised of many interconnected-but-sovereign blockchains that serve different purposes for a variety of communities, and Tendermint will be the leader driving innovation to bring interoperability.

Tendermint the decentralized organization is comprised of all the users and contributors of this technology. Our first prototype network will be a blockchain ledger that represents contributions and interests towards Tendermint.
